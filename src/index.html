RSA

RSA (Rivest-Shamir-Adleman) is one of the most widely used public-key cryptographic systems. It is named after its inventors, Ron Rivest, Adi Shamir, and Leonard Adleman, who introduced it in 1977. RSA is based on the mathematical properties of large prime numbers and their products.
